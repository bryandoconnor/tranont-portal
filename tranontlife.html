FAQ

Q. Can I be a TranontLife agent and still write business with another company?
A. No. The agent agreement states that all agents are “Captive” to TranontLife and to the products we are licensed to sell. Agents writing Life or Annuity business outside of TranontLife are subject to termination from TranontLife. For those with other Lines of Authority, please send in a letter of explanation. Exceptions are granted on a case by case basis.
Q. I just got my license. Now what do I do?
A. The next step is to fill out the New Agent Packet found in the Agent Paperwork section of the TranontLife library. This section has all the paperwork needed to become appointed.
Q. What information do I provide you to become a TranontLife Agent?
A. Everything we need is in the TranontLife New agent Packet located in the Agent Paperwork section. We also provide you with a checklist and video there as well, to help you get started.
Q. Do I need to be licensed as a split agent?
A. Yes. The split agent must be licensed and appointed by the issue date on the commission statement. We cannot hold the commission until you are properly licensed. If you are not properly licensed, that commission will roll up to the next person above you who is properly licensed and appointed.
Q. What if I forgot one signature and the client gives me permission to sign for him/her?
A. Signing on behalf of a customer is considered fraud, and you could lose your license, be fined, or even face prosecution. Never sign for a client, even if he or she has given you permission.
Q. How should I handle non-public information?
A. TranontLife’s policy is that all non-public information should be handled in accordance with privacy laws. Information should be kept safe and shredded when not needed. This does include permanently deleting desktop information to guard against a hacker accessing your digital files.
Q. Do I need E&O Insurance?
A. Yes it is required that every agent have E&O insurance to be appointed and write business. WE offer E&O to TranontLife agents at a discounted rate. The coverage runs from January of the current year to January of the following year. You can find a form to sign up for our E&O in the New Agent Packet. If you choose to get your own E&O, please send us the binder.
Q. What additional training do I need to write annuities?
A. You must complete the product specific training online for each annuity product BEFORE you write the annuity. You only need to complete this training once. You also need to check if the state you are writing in has STATE REQUIRED training. A list is provided in the Agent Paperwork section.
Q. Where do I turn in applications?
A. All applications and delivery receipts need to be emailed to newbusiness@tranont.com. We will scrub them for missing information. If there is something missing, we will attempt to contact the producer to fix the issue. You can also fax applications to TranontLife at 801-384-7143. If you are writing an E-App, please make sure you still send us a copy of the application.
Q. When do commissions pay out?
A. Commissions are paid every Friday.
Q. Where do I find training on the products we sell?
A. In the section titled Ongoing Training you will find past trainings. Trainings are sent directly to your email twice a week.
Q. What licenses do I need for an override commission?
A. Producers must have a license and appointment in the “Hot” states to get an override commission. “Cold” states only require that you are appointed with the insurance carrier to get an override. A list of “Hot” and “Cold” states can be found in the Agent Paperwork section. When calculating commissions, we follow your sponsorship not your placement line.
Q. Do I have to have a license and appointment in every state to get split commissions?
A. Yes. You must have a license and be appointed in every state you are the writing or referring agent in.
Q. Does my state require pre-licensing education to get licensed?
A. This is handled state by state. Each state has different rules for licensing. You can find a list of states that require pre-licensing by logging into Tranont.com and selecting the TranontLife library. The Becoming licensed section has the state requirements. We also have a flyer for ExamFX in that section. Using ExamFX will give you a discount on the education.
Q. How long does it take to become appointed?
A. When we have received all the required documents from the agent, we start the appointing process within 48 hours. Every appointment and company has different timelines for how long it takes. Fidelity and Guaranty can typically have you appointed within 72 hours after everything is sent in. ANICO takes between 10-12 days. If the agent has had bankruptcy in his or her past or has other issues in his or her background, this can increase the wait time or the appointment can be cancelled altogether.
Q. How do I find out if I have been appointed?
A. The Product Provider will either send you a letter in the mail or an email. In the letter, you’ll find your agent ID and a password to the provider’s web site. If an agent thinks he or she should have already been appointed, he or she can call the Product Provider and ask.
Q. When can you start to explain products to your friends and family?
A. While we encourage you to do training with a licensed producer to increase your knowledge, state law prohibits you from talking about products during this training period until you are fully licensed and appointed.
Q. What title can you use as a producer?
A. You should always let the client know what you are licensed to sell life insurance up front. Agents should avoid titles like Financial Advisor, Investment Consultant, 401(k) Rollover Specialist, Financial Planning Specialist, or anything that could lead the client to assuming you are securities licensed (unless you are securities licensed or have earned the designation). You cannot hold yourself out as an expert in areas you are not licensed in.
Q. Can I write an application on myself?
A. TranontLife’s company policy does not allow for the producer to be the agent on their own policy as the writing agent or as the split agent. This is considered rebating of premiums.
Q. Can I give advice on a client’s current investments?
A. As a producer, you may only give advice on products you are licensed to sell. If you are not securities licensed, you cannot perform an analysis or recommend any change in a securities product.
Q. Can I refer securities business to someone that is Securities licensed and still make money?
A. Making any money directly or indirectly from a securities product is not covered by your current license. If you are not securities licensed, it is also illegal.
Q. How do I complete my Continuing Education?
A. Every state has different rules and education requirements. Please refer to your state specific website for approved providers.
Q. When writing new business, besides the Product Provider’s application, what else do I need to turn in to TranontLife?
A. Every application needs a split form and a disclosure turned in with the application. Commissions cannot be paid without them. You can find both in the Forms and Submissions section. Please note that California does has different disclosures.
Q. Whom does the client write a check to?
A. The client should never write a check out to you, the producer. It should always be made out to the Product Provider.
Q. What does becoming a TranontLife Agent do for me?
A. As an agent, you can help make a difference in your friends’ and families’ lives by educating them about finances. While expanding your own financial knowledge, your earned commissions will increase your own financial stability.
Q. How do I participate in the 30% acquisition bonus?
A. To participate you must achieve the Vice President rank on the TranontLife side. You become a Vice President by hitting the qualifications listed on the Compensation Plan found on Tranont’s website.
